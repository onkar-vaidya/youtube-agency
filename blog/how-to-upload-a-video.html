How to Upload a Video to YouTube: A Beginner's Technical Guide

By SooperBlooper Team•July 18, 2025•8 min read

You've finished creating and editing your video, and now it's time to share it with the world. This guide demystifies the technical process of uploading your content to YouTube, ensuring you complete every step correctly for maximum impact.

The Pre-Upload Checklist

Before you even click "Upload," having these items ready will make the process much smoother:

  • Final Video File: Ensure your video is rendered in a common format like MP4.
  • Title, Description, and Tags: Have these written and saved. Need help with tags? Check out our guide on what YouTube tags are and how to use them.
  • Custom Thumbnail: Your thumbnail should be ready to go (1280x720px). This feature is only available after you verify your YouTube account.

The Upload Process, Step-by-Step

All uploads are handled inside your YouTube Studio dashboard. Here's the process:

  1. Click the "Create" button in the top-right corner of YouTube Studio and select "Upload videos."
  2. The "Details" Tab: This is the most important screen. Add your title, description, and upload your custom thumbnail. You'll also set the audience and add your video to playlists here.
  3. The "Video elements" Tab: Add interactive elements like end screens (to promote other videos) and cards (to link to other content).
  4. The "Checks" Tab: YouTube automatically scans your video for potential copyright issues, saving you from problems down the line.
  5. The "Visibility" Tab: Choose who can see your video. You can set it to Private (only you), Unlisted (only people with the link), or Public. We highly recommend using the "Schedule" option to publish your videos at a consistent time.
